Who drives cars?

While gathering data, the first question asked was 'do you drive?'. This question defined how many people my research would apply to and the percentage of people the fuel efficiency was out of. Here is the data collected relating to melbournians driving:


Which mode of transport is most popular in Melbourne?

According to the results on the survey, 78.8% of respondents drive cars on a daily basis while 21.2% don't drive daily. And within those who don't drive daily, +70% of them walk while just under 10% don't travel daily.

What gender drives the most?

With evidence to support, 74% of the daily driving population is male while only 23% is female and 3% is 'other'. According to the 'Workplace Gender Equality Agency', the workforce is only made of 53.8% male workers so there appears no correlation between working and driving. However, the construction industry is made of 88.3% of males, this means there could be a correlation between construction and driving.
