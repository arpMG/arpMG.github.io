Are they cost efficient?

The cost efficiency of day to day cars and electric cars was difficult to calculate due to varying electric prices. These prices for electricity were based of the average of electrical company plans found online while the day to day fuel prices were averaged from respondents.


Efficiency for each fuel?

Statistically, petrol users save about 30% more money then diesel users, however an electrical car would cost almost 50% less to fuel the same amount. Although there is only a $9.65 difference between petrol and electrical, that would save $501.80 a year if fueling once a week.

Average km?

This data represents how many average km(s) people traveled per day and if 200km per week is too little. Results show an average of about 300km per week, meaning 100km less then what was initially thought.
