Best Prevention of Rubbish

Reuse, Recycle, Reduce and Recover

Re-use surplus food within the human food chain.

  • If food is not fit for human consumption, the next best option is to divert it for livestock feed, conserving resources that would otherwise be used to produce commercial feedstuff.
Reducing food wastage in the first place.
  • Improved rice-storage bags in the Philippines have helped cut losses by 15% and in West Africa, use of solar dryers to extend the shelf life of fruit is showing promise in reducing post-harvest losses.
Where re-use is not possible, recycling, and recovery should be pursued.
What can you do at home to reduce waste?
  • Write a shopping list based on a weekly meal plan.
  • Store food correctly. This useful guide will show you how to increase the shelf life of a huge number of products
  • Reuse left overs for lunch or as the basis for another meal.

The Alternative Plastic Bag

Boomerang Bags

Boomerang bags are free, and they lead to greater than 62,000 kilograms of waste being diverting from landfill. It was found that between 6,350 and 245,000 metric tons of plastic is estimated to float on the ocean's surface. Therefore by using Boomerang Bags you can help to reduce this figure and help save the environment and the animals being harmed.

Conclusion

The survey showed there is a clear correlation that awareness, reward and punishment would lead to a reduction in rubbish. It also showed that people are not looking for solutions and are often not recognizing the extent of the issue but may be willing to implement changes to reduce their rubbish if it was organized for them, if they were given a reward or were punished. Therefore, the hypothesis is partly correct as it shows those surveyed are willing to reduce their rubbish however, it is not all about reward as other factors motivate people.
